STEP 1: Creation of a Melody Story

Creating a Melody story on the Melody website using knowledge graphs can be an engaging way to present information and explore a specific topic. In our case, the chosen themes were "The Beatles" and “Joy Division”, we aimed to showcase their history, members, and influences within a specific time period.

To begin, we created a Melody account on the website and started the process of crafting our data story. By selecting the theme as "The Beatles" and “Joy Division” we set the focus of our project on these 2 iconic bands. Our hypothesis was that both bands had a significant impact on the mentality and culture of people worldwide.

To support our hypothesis, we utilized SPARQL queries to search the WIKIDATA database. WIKIDATA is a collaborative knowledge graph that contains structured data from various sources, including Wikipedia. By referencing pre-established codes within WIKIDATA (such as country codes), we constructed queries following the examples provided by your professor.

After checking the validity of our queries on WIKIDATA, we implemented them on the Melody website. The website offers various templates and visualization options like count, line charts, doughnut charts, tables, etc. Once our queries retrieved the desired information accurately, we arranged and presented the data in a coherent storyline.

Throughout the process, we used different types of queries based on the specific information we required. For instance, we explored the bands’ histories, their personal lives, and their fame. Each time, we followed the process of checking the relevant code on WIKIDATA, constructing a search query to extract the desired data, verifying the obtained information, and finally incorporating it into the Melody website. By following this methodology, we were able to create a Melody story centered around "The Beatles" and “Joy Division” by utilizing knowledge graphs and the resources provided by WIKIDATA.

STEP 2: Writing an article

Once we completed the story on the Melody website, our next step was to gather academic papers that would reinforce our hypothesis and provide a deeper understanding of the theme. These scholarly sources would serve as a foundation for writing a comprehensive articles about "The Beatles” and “Joy Division” and their cultural impact.

Our search for bibliographic content led us to discover a wealth of valuable resources that allowed us to delve into the fascinating story of this iconic bands. After thoroughly studying the material and gaining profound insights, we embarked on the process of crafting our article.

Drawing from the knowledge acquired through extensive research, we delved into the realm of bands within this genre and explored the profound influence they exerted on society. The resulting article can be accessed on the designated "article" page of this website, presenting a thorough analysis of the impact of "The Beatles" and “Joy Division” and their genres on a national scale.

Challenges

Undertaking this project presented us with novel and unfamiliar territory. The tools we encountered were new to us, and understanding their functionality proved to be a struggle. Additionally, we encountered various challenges while working with the Polifonia Corpus. We encountered difficulties in finding sufficient data, and there were instances where the website indicated the presence of data, but it failed to display any relevant information.

Working with WIKIDATA also posed its own set of challenges. Despite conducting thorough research on the main website, we often faced discrepancies when executing queries. The information retrieved did not always align with our expectations, and there were instances of incomplete or missing data.

The dearth of data within the provided tools was significant enough to prompt us to change our project theme. Initially, we intended to compare Russian and Ukrainian songs, but the limited information available in the Polifonia Corpus and the challenges faced with WIKIDATA queries made it unfeasible. Creating the Melody story proved to be a demanding task. It required numerous hours of dedicated effort to comprehend its workings fully. Some queries that yielded results on the WIKIDATA query interface did not produce the same outcome on the Melody website. Consequently, we had to modify our queries multiple times or, in some cases, abandon certain information altogether due to the query's inability to be processed. Furthermore, developing the HTML files posed complexities as we lacked familiarity with this aspect of web development. While this project may be straightforward for individuals experienced with the tools we employed, for us, it presented a steep learning curve.

Conclusion

Despite the challenges encountered throughout this project, it provided us with a valuable opportunity to explore the realms of music, data, and technology. We acquired new information, honed our skills, and expanded our understanding in these domains. While the project diverged from our previous experiences, it proved to be an enriching endeavor, enabling us to grow professionally and intellectually.
